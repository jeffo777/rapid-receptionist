### Essential Services Trade Strategy

The 13 trades are:

| **Appliance Repair** | ★★★☆☆ | Broken fridge (food spoilage), washing machine leak, oven failure |

#### Why This Strategy Is Strong

**1. Non-discretionary demand drives urgency.**
The cornerstone of this trade selection is that these services are **not optional**. When a pipe bursts at midnight, the homeowner isn't comparison-shopping for weeks — they're calling *right now*. When a business owner's power goes out, they aren't budgeting for next quarter — they need an electrician *today*. This non-discretionary demand creates a constant, recession-resistant stream of inbound calls that makes an AI receptionist genuinely indispensable.

**2. Emergency work has the highest missed-call cost.**
The cost of a missed call in these trades is disproportionately high because emergency and urgent jobs command premium rates. A missed lockout call at midnight could be a £150-£300 job. A missed total power outage call is a £200+ emergency callout. A missed burst pipe call is a £200-£500 job. The ROI argument for an AI receptionist — "one captured emergency call pays for a year of service" — is uniquely powerful for these trades.

**3. These tradesmen literally cannot answer the phone.**
Unlike office-based businesses, these tradesmen work with their hands in conditions that physically prevent them from answering calls: up ladders (roofers, glaziers), inside consumer units (electricians), under boilers (gas engineers, plumbers), in confined drainage spaces, or in active security situations (locksmiths). The AI receptionist isn't a nice-to-have — it solves a genuine, daily operational problem.

**4. 24/7 demand maximises AI value.**
Pipes burst at 2am. People get locked out at midnight. Gas leaks happen on Christmas Day. These trades have genuine 24/7 demand, which means the AI receptionist provides value during hours that no human receptionist would cover without significant additional cost.

**5. Customers are emotionally motivated.**
A homeowner calling about a gas leak, a lockout, or a burst pipe is not in a calm, rational state. They're stressed, sometimes frightened, and highly motivated to book the first competent responder. An AI that answers instantly, speaks calmly, and captures their details is exactly what they need — and exactly what the tradesman is paying for.

#### Evaluation: Is This the Right Set of Trades?

**Verdict: Yes — with one refinement.**

The current 13 trades represent an excellent core selection for Rapid Receptionist. They share the essential characteristics that make an AI receptionist high-value: non-discretionary demand, emergency urgency, physical inability to answer calls, and 24/7 need.

The selection also avoids the trap of targeting trades where the work is primarily discretionary or planned — such as painters & decorators, landscapers, or interior designers — where the missed-call cost is lower and the urgency argument is weaker. Those trades can be added later (see §4.4 Growth Opportunities) but are a lower priority for launch.

The one potential addition worth considering for launch is **Heating Engineers** as a separate category from Gas Engineers, to capture the large overlap market that doesn't self-identify as "gas" but searches for "heating engineer" or "central heating repair." However, this can also be handled through keyword targeting within the existing Gas Engineers pages.
